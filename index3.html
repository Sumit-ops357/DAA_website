Smart water management

SDG 11.5: By 2030, significantly reduce the number of deaths and the number of people affected and substantially decrease the direct economic losses relative to global gross domestic product caused by disasters, including water-related disasters, with a focus on protecting the poor and people in vulnerable situations

This target emphasizes the need for disaster risk reduction, which directly relates to the role of smart water management systems in reducing the impacts of water-related disasters, including floods..

SDG 11.6: By 2030, reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities, including by paying special attention to air quality and municipal and other waste management

A smart water management system that optimizes water usage helps in reducing the environmental impact of urban areas by minimizing waste and ensuring that water resources are managed efficiently

Urban farming and food supplying

SDG 11.3: By 2030, enhance inclusive and sustainable urbanization and capacity for participatory, integrated and sustainable human settlement planning and management in all countries

Urban farming directly contributes to this target by promoting sustainable land use within cities, enhancing food security, and integrating agriculture into urban environments. It supports cities in adapting to growing populations while improving urban food systems.
